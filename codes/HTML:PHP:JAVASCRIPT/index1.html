Transcriptome and Epigenome Landscapes during Embryonic Hematopoiesis

Hematopoietic stem cell (HSC) ontogeny is accompanied by dynamic changes in gene expression. We performed RNA-Seq and ChIP-Seq to define the transcriptomes and epigenomes of cells representing key developmental stages of HSC ontogeny in the mouse embryo. By linking enhancers to their target promoters using a novel computational algorithm, we constructed developmental-stage-specific transcriptional regulatory networks during HSC ontogeny. Our computational analyses predicted known transcriptional regulators for the endothelial-to-hematopoietic transition, validating our overall approach, and identified putative novel transcription factors whose expression correlates with the emergence of pre-HSCs. Our data and computational analyses provide a useful resource for uncovering new markers and regulators of HSC formation from the major arteries.
